MSTR ⇄ BTC Decision Engine

Rational mNAV — valuing the wrapper in Bitcoin terms against the self-custody hurdle. Self-custodied BTC is the benchmark: one coin stays one coin, a 0% Bitcoin-denominated return. The tool asks whether owning MSTR provides superior returns compared to holding self-custody Bitcoin, after a counterparty-risk hurdle. It runs a Monte Carlo over the Bitcoin price — under your choice of three models (power law, diminishing returns, or GBM), each anchored to its own estimate of today's fair value — and the short policy rate (SOFR), then propagates Strategy's full capital structure (an endogenous floating-preferred dividend, convertibles resolving at their put dates, a premium-scaled accretive equity program, and a strict financing waterfall) to a fair premium: the mNAV at which the equity beats self-custody half the time. Here mNAV is Strategy's equity market capitalization — the MSTR share price times diluted shares outstanding — divided by the total dollar value of all the Bitcoin the company holds. The market and balance-sheet fields come pre-filled with a starting snapshot — overwrite them with current values — and your job is to set the horizon, the hurdle, and which Bitcoin model you believe.
